Bhagat Singh

The man who made the saying true:
"Life should be great rather than long"

Here's the timeline of the Bhagat Singh's life:

  • 1907 - Born in Banga, Punjab, now in Pakistan.

  • 1919 - Visited the site of Jallianwla Bagh massacre.

  • 1921 - Was among those in his village who welcomed protesters against the killing of a large number of unarmed people at Gurudwara Nankana Sahib.

  • 1922 - Became disillusioned with Mahatma Gandhi's philosophy of non-violence after he called off the non-cooperation movement.

  • 1923 -Joined the National College in Lahore, where he also participated in extra-curricular activities like the dramatics society.

  • 1926 - Inspired by the Young Italy movement of Giuseppe Mazzini, founded the Indian socialist youth organisation Naujawan Bharat Sabha.

  • 1927 - Police became concerned with Singh's influence on youths and arrested him on the pretext that he had been involved in a bombing that had taken place in Lahore in October 1926.

  • 1928 - British government set up the Simon Commision, and there were protests across the country and the Superindent of police, James A. Scott , ordered the police to lathi charge in which Lala Lajpat Rai succumbed to death. Even his ideologies were different from Lala Rajpat Rai, he had respect for him. Along with revolutionaries like Shivaram Rajguru, Sukhdev Thapar , and Chandrashekhar Azad he conspired to kill Scott. However, in case of mistaken identity , the plotters shot John P. Saunders, an Assistant Superintendant of Police.

  • 1929 - Influenced by Auguste Vaillant, a French anarchist who had bombed the Chamber of Deputies in Paris, Singh's plan was to explode a bomb inside the Central Legislative Assembly. The bombs had been designed not to kill. Even all of them could escaped from this,they stayed shouting the solgan "Inquilab Zindabad!" ("Long Live the revolution"). The actual intention was to use the court appearances as a stage to publicise their cause.

  • 1931 - Along with Rajguru and Sukhdev, he was hanged on 23rd March.
